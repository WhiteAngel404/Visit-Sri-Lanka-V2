UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Sri Lanka boasts 8 UNESCO World Heritage Sites - 6 cultural and 2 natural

UNESCO 1982

Ancient City of Sigiriya

Central Province

A 5th-century rock fortress rising 200m above the plains. Features remarkable frescoes, water gardens, and the famous Lion's Paws entrance.

Lion Rock Frescoes Water Gardens Mirror Wall
$30 USD 3-4 hours June-March
UNESCO 1982

Sacred City of Anuradhapura

North Central Province

First ancient capital with the world's oldest historically authenticated tree (Sri Maha Bodhi) and massive dagobas dating to 4th century BC.

Sri Maha Bodhi Ruwanwelisaya Jetavanaramaya Abhayagiri
4th Century BC 1-2 days Year-round
UNESCO 1982

Ancient City of Polonnaruwa

North Central Province

Second oldest kingdom with remarkable Gal Vihara Buddha statues, royal palaces, and Parakrama Samudra lake.

Gal Vihara Royal Palace Parakrama Samudra Lankatilaka
$25 USD Half day Year-round
UNESCO 1991

Rangiri Dambulla Cave Temple

Central Province

Largest cave temple complex in Sri Lanka with over 150 Buddha statues and stunning wall paintings spanning 2,000 years.

5 Caves 150+ Statues 2,100m² Paintings Golden Temple
$15 USD 1-2 hours Year-round
UNESCO 1988

Sacred City of Kandy

Central Province

Last ancient capital home to the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, one of Buddhism's most sacred shrines.

Temple of Tooth Kandy Lake Peradeniya Gardens Esala Perahera
3hrs from Colombo 2-3 days Year-round
UNESCO 1988

Old Town of Galle

Southern Province

Best-preserved colonial sea fortress in South Asia, built by Portuguese in 1588 with Dutch influences.

Galle Fort Dutch Church Cricket Ground Unawatuna
2hrs from Colombo 1-2 days Nov-April
UNESCO Natural

Sinharaja Forest Reserve

Southwest Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka's last viable area of primary tropical rainforest. Home to endemic wildlife found nowhere else on Earth.

Endemic Birds Leopard Giant Squirrel Trekking
LKR 2,000 Full day Jan-March
UNESCO Natural

Central Highlands

Central Province

Encompasses Horton Plains, Knuckles Range, and World's End viewpoint. Misty mountains with unique ecosystems.

World's End Horton Plains Knuckles Range Tea Plantations
1,800-2,500m Full day Dec-April

National Parks & Wildlife

Sri Lanka has 26 national parks home to leopards, elephants, and endemic species

Best Safari

Yala National Park

Southeast Sri Lanka • 979 sq km

Sri Lanka's most famous wildlife sanctuary. Home to the world's highest leopard density, elephants, sloth bears, and 200+ bird species.

Leopard Elephant Sloth Bear Crocodile
From $50 3-4 hours Feb-July
Elephant Haven

Udawalawe National Park

South-Central • 306 sq km

Sri Lanka's premier elephant park. Herds of 50+ elephants gather around the Udawalawe reservoir. Best for guaranteed elephant sightings.

Elephant Herds Sambar Deer Water Buffalo Crocodiles
From $40 3-4 hours Year-round
World's Largest

Minneriya Elephant Gathering

North Central Province

Witness the world's largest gathering of Asian elephants during the dry season (June-September). Up to 300 elephants gather!

Elephant Gathering 300+ Elephants Safari Photography
From $35 3-4 hours June-Sept
Ancient Park

Wilpattu National Park

Northwest Coast • 1,317 sq km

One of the oldest and largest parks. Famous for 'villus' - natural lakes surrounded by grassy plains. Leopards, sloth bears, and elephants.

Villus (Lakes) Leopard Sloth Bear Elephant
From $40 3-4 hours Feb-Oct
Hiking Paradise

Horton Plains National Park

Central Highlands • 3,169 ha

UNESCO World Heritage Site. Only national park where you can walk. Famous for World's End - a 900m cliff with stunning views.

World's End Baker's Falls Sambar Deer Endemic Birds
$30 USD 3-4 hours Dec-April
Marine Park

Hikkaduwa National Park

Southwest Coast • 114 ha

One of Sri Lanka's two marine parks. Features fringing coral reefs, seagrass beds, and sea turtles. Snorkeling paradise.

Coral Reefs Sea Turtles Snorkeling Diving
LKR 500 Half day Nov-April

Beautiful Beaches

1,340 km of coastline with beaches for surfing, swimming, whale watching, and relaxation

South & West Coast (Best: Nov-April)

Best Swimming

Unawatuna Beach

6km from Galle

Most photographed beach in Sri Lanka. Curved horseshoe bay with calm reef-protected waters. Perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

4.8/5 Snorkeling Restaurants
Whale Watching

Mirissa Beach

Matara District

Beautiful crescent beach famous for whale watching. Blue whales, dolphins, and sea turtles Nov-April. Vibrant beach bar scene.

4.9/5 Whale Tours Nightlife
Best Surfing

Weligama Beach

Matara District

Sri Lanka's best beginner surfing beach. Long open bay with gentle rolling waves. Many surf schools available.

4.6/5 Surfing Lessons
Diving

Hikkaduwa Beach

Galle District

Most developed beach resort town. Famous for surfing, turtle conservation, and coral reefs accessible from shore.

4.5/5 Diving Turtles
Water Sports

Bentota Beach

Kalutara District

Water sports hub with jet skiing, parasailing, and boat trips. Family-friendly with calm waters and resorts.

4.4/5 Jet Ski Ayurveda
Backpacker

Hiriketiya Beach

Near Dikwella

Small horseshoe bay with gentle waves. Popular with surfers and backpackers. Laid-back atmosphere with surf camps.

4.8/5 Surfing Yoga

East Coast (Best: May-Oct)

Surf Mecca

Arugam Bay

Ampara District

World-renowned surf destination. Consistent waves May-October. Laid-back beach town with yoga, lagoon safaris, and Yala nearby.

4.7/5 Surfing Yoga
Snorkeling

Nilaveli Beach

15km from Trincomalee

Long ivory beach with crystal-clear waters. Perfect for swimming and snorkeling. Access to Pigeon Island Marine Park.

4.8/5 Pigeon Island Swimming
Family

Pasikudah Beach

35km from Batticaloa

Shallow turquoise waters extending far from shore. Ideal for families with children and non-swimmers. Calmest east coast beach.

4.6/5 Safe Swimming Watersports
Whale Watching

Trincomalee Beach

Eastern Province

Natural deep-water harbor with stunning beaches. Excellent whale watching (Feb-April), diving, and colonial history.

4.6/5 Whale Tours Diving

Spectacular Waterfalls

Sri Lanka's central highlands are home to numerous stunning waterfalls

2nd Tallest

Diyaluma Waterfall

Koslanda, near Ella

Sri Lanka's 2nd tallest waterfall at 220m. Famous for natural infinity pools at the top where you can swim. Multi-tiered cascades.

220m Height Swimming Pools Hiking Photography
LKR 350 June-Sept
Tallest

Bambarakanda Falls

Haputale

Sri Lanka's tallest waterfall at 263m. Located in the Uva highlands near Haputale. Stunning surrounding tea plantations.

263m Height Tea Country Hiking Nature
Dec-April
Easy Access

Ravana Ella Falls

Ella-Wellawaya Road

Popular roadside waterfall near Ella. Named after King Ravana from Ramayana. Easily accessible with nearby cave to explore.

25m Height Roadside Ravana Cave Free
Year-round
Nuwara Eliya

Ramboda Falls

Nuwara Eliya

Beautiful 109m waterfall in the Ramboda area. Part of the Panna Oya stream. Scenic views with tea estates nearby.

109m Height Tea Estates Photography
Year-round
Badulla

Dunhinda Waterfall

Near Badulla

Beautiful 64m waterfall with a misty appearance. Popular hiking destination with scenic views along the path.

64m Height Hiking Misty Views
Year-round
Famous

Bopath Ella

Kuruwita

Famous waterfall shaped like a bo tree (ficus religiosa). Immortalized in Sri Lankan poetry. Easy access from main road.

30m Height Bo Tree Shape Folklore
Year-round

Sacred Temples & Religious Sites

Buddhist, Hindu, and colonial religious sites across Sri Lanka

Most Sacred

Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic

Kandy

One of Buddhism's most sacred shrines, housing the relic of Buddha's tooth. Built in 16th century within the royal palace complex.

LKR 1,500 Dress Code
UNESCO

Dambulla Cave Temple

Dambulla

Largest cave temple complex with 150 Buddha statues and paintings spanning 2,000 years. Sacred pilgrimage site since 1st century BC.

$15 USD 1-2 hours
Ancient

Mihintale

Near Anuradhapura

Where Buddhism was introduced to Sri Lanka in 246 BC. Sacred pilgrimage site with ancient ruins and stunning views from the summit.

LKR 500 Climbing
UNESCO

Gal Vihara

Polonnaruwa

Rock temple with four Buddha statues carved from granite. The reclining Buddha is a masterpiece of ancient Sri Lankan art.

Included Photography
Hindu

Koneswaram Temple

Trincomalee

Hindu temple on a promontory overlooking the sea. One of the 18 important Shakta temples dedicated to goddess Parvati.

Dress Code Hindu
Hindu

Nallur Kandaswamy Temple

Jaffna

Major Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Skanda. One of the most important Hindu shrines in Sri Lanka with Dravidian architecture.

Dress Code Hindu

Adventure Activities

From whale watching to mountain hiking - endless adventures await

Must-Do

Whale Watching

Mirissa / Trincomalee

Watch blue whales, sperm whales, and dolphins. Mirissa: Nov-April. Trincomalee: Feb-April. Success rate over 95%.

$50-80 3-5 hours
Wildlife

Wildlife Safari

Yala / Wilpattu / Udawalawe

Game drives to spot leopards, elephants, sloth bears, and more. Yala has world's highest leopard density.

$40-100 3-6 hours
Surfing

Surfing

South & East Coasts

Beginner-friendly in Weligama/Mirissa (Nov-April). World-class at Arugam Bay (May-Oct). Lessons for all levels.

$15-50/lesson All Levels
Diving

Scuba Diving

Hikkaduwa / Trincomalee / Pigeon Island

Explore coral reefs, shipwrecks, and marine turtles. Pigeon Island has pristine reefs. All certification levels welcome.

$60-120 Full day
Hiking

Mountain Hiking

Ella / Nuwara Eliya / Adam's Peak

Ella Rock, Little Adam's Peak, World's End, and sacred Adam's Peak pilgrimage. Options for all fitness levels.

2-6 hours Various
Scenic

Scenic Train Journey

Kandy to Ella

One of world's most beautiful train journeys. Passes through tea plantations, mountains, and cloud forests. 6-7 hours.

$3-15 6-7 hours

Best Time to Visit

Plan your trip based on what you want to experience

South & West Coasts

Best: November to April

Calm seas, sunny weather, perfect for beaches, whale watching in Mirissa, and surfing.

27-32°C Beach Season

East Coast

Best: May to October

When south is monsoon season, east coast shines. Surfing at Arugam Bay, diving at Trincomalee.

28-33°C Surf Season

Hill Country

Best: December to April

Clear skies for World's End, Adam's Peak pilgrimage season. Scenic train journeys are spectacular.

13-22°C Hiking Season

Wildlife Watching

Best: Year-round, varies by park
