Culture & Heritage

Discover the rich traditions of Sri Lanka

A Tapestry of Traditions

Sri Lanka's culture is a rich blend of indigenous traditions fused with influences from Indian, Arab, and European settlers. The island's history spans over 2,500 years, with Buddhism playing a central role in shaping its cultural identity.

From ancient temples and colonial forts to traditional dance and vibrant festivals, Sri Lanka offers a cultural experience like no other. The warmth and hospitality of the Sri Lankan people, combined with their deep-rooted traditions, create an unforgettable experience for visitors.

Major Festivals

Experience the vibrant celebrations throughout the year

Kandy Esala Perahera

July / August

One of the most spectacular religious processions in the world, held annually in honor of the Sacred Tooth Relic of Lord Buddha. The festival features elaborately decorated elephants, traditional dancers, drummers, and fire performers parading through the streets of Kandy for ten nights.

Learn More

Sinhala & Tamil New Year (Aluth Avurudda)

April 13-14

The traditional New Year celebration marking the end of the harvest season. Families observe rituals at auspicious times, prepare special sweetmeats, visit relatives, and engage in traditional games. The festival symbolizes prosperity and togetherness.

Learn More

Vesak (Buddha Purnima)

May (Full Moon)

The most important Buddhist festival celebrating the birth, enlightenment, and passing of Lord Buddha. Streets are decorated with colorful lanterns (vesak koodu), pandals display elaborate scenes from Buddha's life, and free food (dana) is distributed to all.

Learn More

Deepavali (Diwali)

October / November

The Hindu festival of lights celebrated by the Tamil community. Homes are decorated with oil lamps and colorful rangoli designs, special sweets are prepared, and families come together to celebrate the victory of light over darkness.

Traditional Arts & Dance

Discover centuries-old performing arts

Kandyan Dance

Classical dance form from the hill country featuring intricate footwork, hand gestures, and acrobatic movements. The dancers wear elaborate traditional costumes with ornate jewelry.

Traditional Mask Making

Ambalangoda is famous for traditional devil masks used in exorcism rituals and healing ceremonies. These colorful wooden masks are hand-carved and painted using age-old techniques.

Traditional Drumming

The "Gata Bera" (drum ensemble) is essential for any celebration. Different drums like the Geta Bera, Yak Bera, and Davula produce unique rhythms for various ceremonies and dances.

Batik & Textiles

Sri Lankan batik features intricate designs with vibrant colors inspired by nature. Traditional handloom textiles, especially from areas like Dumbara, showcase beautiful patterns and craftsmanship.

Cultural Etiquette

Tips for respectful travel

Dress Code

Cover shoulders and knees when visiting temples and religious sites. Remove shoes and hats before entering temples.

Religious Sites

Don't turn your back to Buddha statues for photos. Walk clockwise around stupas and Buddhist sites.

Greetings

The traditional greeting is placing palms together in a prayer-like gesture and saying "Ayubowan" (may you live long).

Dining

Eating with right hand is common, though cutlery is widely available. Wait for elders to start eating before you begin.
