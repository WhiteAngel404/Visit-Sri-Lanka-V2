Travel Guide

Everything you need to know for your trip

Best Time to Visit

Plan your trip according to Sri Lanka's two monsoon seasons

December - March

Best for: South and West Coast beaches

  • Colombo, Galle, Mirissa
  • Dry and sunny weather
  • Perfect for whale watching
  • Peak tourist season

April - September

Best for: East Coast and Cultural Triangle

  • Arugam Bay, Pasikudah, Trincomalee
  • Ideal for surfing
  • Sigiriya, Anuradhapura visits
  • Fewer crowds

Hill Country

Best for: Kandy, Nuwara Eliya, Ella

  • Visit year-round
  • Cooler climate (15-25°C)
  • Best: January-March
  • Avoid heavy rain in Oct-Nov

Shoulder Seasons

October-November and May-June

  • Intermittent rain possible
  • Lower prices
  • Fewer tourists
  • Still good for travel

Visa Requirements

Entry requirements for Sri Lanka

Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA)

Most visitors require an ETA to enter Sri Lanka. The application process is simple and can be completed online before your trip.

Important Information

  • Apply online at eta.gov.lk
  • Passport must be valid for 6 months
  • Approval usually within 24-48 hours
  • Print ETA approval to show at immigration

Visa Fees:

  • Tourist ETA (30 days) $35-50 USD
  • Business ETA $40-60 USD
  • Transit (2 days) Free
  • SAARC countries Discounted rates
Apply for ETA Online

Pro Tips

  • Apply at least 2 weeks before travel
  • Double-check passport number on application
  • Keep a digital copy of ETA approval
  • Visa can be extended at Immigration office in Colombo
  • Extension fee: approx $60-100 for 2-6 months

On Arrival Visa

Some nationalities can obtain visa on arrival, but we strongly recommend applying for ETA online in advance to avoid delays.

Currency & Money

Managing your finances in Sri Lanka

Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR)

The official currency is the Sri Lankan Rupee. Banknotes: 20, 50, 100, 500, 1000, 2000, 5000.

Approximate rates:

  • 1 USD ≈ 300 LKR
  • 1 EUR ≈ 330 LKR
  • 1 GBP ≈ 380 LKR

Cards & ATMs

  • Major cards accepted in hotels, restaurants
  • ATMs available in cities and towns
  • Inform bank before traveling
  • Keep some cash for rural areas
  • UnionPay widely accepted

Currency Exchange

  • Banks offer best rates
  • Exchange at airport (lower rate)
  • Authorized money changers available
  • Keep exchange receipts
  • Re-exchange before departure

Tipping Guidelines

  • Restaurants: 10% (check if included)
  • Hotel porters: 100-200 LKR
  • Drivers: 500-1000 LKR/day
  • Guides: 1000+ LKR/day
  • Round up for tuk-tuks

Getting Around

Transportation options in Sri Lanka

Train Travel

The most scenic way to travel, especially Kandy to Ella. Book 1st or 2nd class observation cars for the best views.

$2-25 Most Scenic

Public Buses

Extensive network covering most destinations. Very affordable but can be crowded. Both CTB (red) and private buses available.

$0.50-5 Most Affordable

Tuk-Tuks

Three-wheelers perfect for short distances. Always negotiate price beforehand or insist on meter. Download PickMe or Uber apps.

$0.50-10 Most Convenient

Private Driver

Hiring a car with driver ($50-80/day) offers flexibility and comfort. Drivers often act as guides and know the best routes.

$50-80/day Most Comfortable

Health & Safety

Stay safe and healthy during your trip

Vaccinations

  • Hepatitis A & B recommended
  • Typhoid (if visiting rural areas)
  • Tetanus (ensure up-to-date)
  • Japanese Encephalitis (for long stays)
  • Rabies (for wildlife exposure risk)

General Safety

  • Sri Lanka is generally safe
  • Take usual travel precautions
  • Avoid political demonstrations
  • Use hotel safes for valuables
  • Emergency: 119 (Police)

Medications

  • Bring prescription medicines
  • Anti-malarial if visiting north/east
  • Anti-diarrheal medication
  • Oral rehydration salts
  • Insect repellent (DEET)

Food & Water

  • Drink bottled/filtered water
  • Avoid ice in drinks (unless purified)
  • Eat at reputable restaurants
  • Peel fruits before eating
  • Street food: choose busy stalls

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from travelers

What is the typical daily budget for Sri Lanka?

Budget travelers can manage on $30-50/day (hostels, local food, buses). Mid-range: $80-150/day (3-star hotels, restaurants, private transport). Luxury: $200+/day (boutique hotels, fine dining, private guides).

Is it safe to travel alone in Sri Lanka?

Yes, Sri Lanka is generally safe for solo travelers, including women. Exercise normal precautions, dress modestly at religious sites, and use reputable transport options. The local people are friendly and helpful.

Do I need a SIM card? Where can I get one?

Yes, highly recommended! Get a tourist SIM at the airport (Dialog or Mobitel) for about $5-10 with data. Coverage is excellent throughout the island. You'll need your passport to purchase.

What should I pack for Sri Lanka?

Light cotton clothes, modest attire for temples (cover shoulders/knees), comfortable walking shoes, swimwear, sun hat, sunscreen, insect repellent, light jacket for hill country, rain jacket if visiting during monsoon, and power adapter (Type D/M plugs).

Is the tap water safe to drink?

No, tap water is not recommended for drinking. Use bottled water (widely available), filtered water from hotels, or carry a water purification bottle. Avoid ice unless you're sure it's made from purified water.

Can I use US dollars in Sri Lanka?

USD is accepted in some tourist areas and larger hotels, but Sri Lankan Rupees (LKR) are required for most transactions. Exchange money at banks or use ATMs to withdraw LKR. Major tourist activities and hotels may accept cards.
