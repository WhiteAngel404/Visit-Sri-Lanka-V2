Sri Lankan Cuisine

A flavorful journey through spice and tradition

The Spice Island

Sri Lankan cuisine is a tantalizing fusion of flavors influenced by Portuguese, Dutch, British, Indian, Arab, and Malay settlers. Known for its bold use of spices, fresh seafood, and coconut, every meal is a celebration of taste.

Rice is the staple, served with an array of curries (meat, fish, vegetable), sambols, and mallungs. The famous "rice and curry" spread can include up to 10 different dishes, creating a feast of flavors, textures, and colors.

Must-Try Dishes

Essential Sri Lankan foods you shouldn't miss

Medium

Rice and Curry

Main Dish

The heart of Sri Lankan cuisine - steamed rice served with multiple curries including dhal, fish/chicken, vegetables, and sambols.

Mild

Hoppers (Appa)

Breakfast / Snack

Bowl-shaped pancakes made from fermented rice flour and coconut milk. Try egg hoppers with a runny egg in the center!

Spicy

Kottu Roti

Street Food

Chopped roti stir-fried with vegetables, egg, and meat with spices. The rhythmic chopping sound is iconic to Sri Lankan streets.

Medium

Lamprais

Dutch Burgher Cuisine

Rice boiled in stock with mixed meat curry, frikkadels, and sambol, wrapped in banana leaf and baked.

Mild

String Hoppers (Idiyappam)

Breakfast

Steamed rice noodle nests served with coconut sambol and curry. Light, fluffy, and perfect for breakfast.

Hot

Pol Sambol

Side Dish

Fresh coconut relish with chili, onion, and lime. The quintessential accompaniment to every Sri Lankan meal.

Sour Spicy

Fish Ambul Thiyal

Fish Curry

Sour fish curry with goraka (dried Garcinia) giving it a unique tangy flavor. Keeps for days without refrigeration.

Sweet

Watalappam

Dessert

Rich coconut custard with jaggery, cashews, and spices. A traditional dessert during celebrations.

Beverages

Quench your thirst the Sri Lankan way

Ceylon Tea

World-famous black tea grown in the central highlands. Enjoy with milk and sugar or plain with lemon.

King Coconut (Thambili)

Refreshing orange coconut water, nature's perfect electrolyte drink sold by roadside vendors.

Wood Apple Juice

Unique sour-sweet fruit juice blended with jaggery and water. An acquired taste loved by locals.

Arrack

Traditional Sri Lankan spirit distilled from coconut flower sap. The national alcoholic beverage.

Dining Etiquette

Tips for eating like a local

01

Eat with Right Hand

Traditionally, Sri Lankans eat with their right hand. The left hand is considered unclean.

02

Wait for Elders

Allow elders to start eating before you begin. It's a sign of respect.

03

Try Everything

When invited to someone's home, try a little of everything offered. Refusing completely may be considered rude.

04

Leave a Little

Leave a small amount of food on your plate to show your host that you've been well-fed.
